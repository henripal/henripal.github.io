' reference: ! 'Parrondo, Juan M. R., Jordan M. Horowitz, and Takahiro Sagawa. Thermodynamics of Information. Nature Physics 11, no. 2 (February 2015)' ---

Using HTML5/JS based frameworks for scientific presentations is a perfect fit. The main advantages over traditional, Powerpoint-based slides are overwhelming: your presentations are instantly web ready. You can use JS to embed interactive simulations to encourage audience participation. You can even create a text or email-based real-time poll!

After some browsing I used reveal.js, and would highly recommend it. It's easy to use and very flexible. I prepared this presentation using the reveal.js framework in roughly one morning, with basic html and css skills.
